What is Net Neutrality?Keeping the Internet open!

Net neutrality is the principle that Internet service providers must treat all data on the Internet the same, and not discriminate or charge differently by user, content, website, platform, application, etc. Under these principles, internet service providers are unable to intentionally block, slow down or charge money for specific websites and online content.

Why do we need it?Open access to all!

Without Net Neutrality, Internet service providers cna block certain websites just because they don't like them! For example, Comcast can block and news sites other than news channels they directly own. Obviously, this is a problem. Without NN, ISPs can also slow down your Internet connection to certain sites (like Netflix), and force you to pay more for faster speeds (to prevent buffering).
